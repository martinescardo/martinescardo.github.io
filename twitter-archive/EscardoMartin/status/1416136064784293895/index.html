In MLTT, "∃x:X,A(x)" means, by convention, the type "Σx:X,A(x)". An element of this type is a pair (x,a) with x:X and a:A(x). So to prove "∃x:X,A(x)" means to exhibit a witness x:X and give a proof a:A(x).

Now the nice thing is the following. In any topos, there is "Σ" as well as "∃". They are different things, but they are related by the equation

(∃x:X,A(x)) = ∥Σx:X,A(x)∥.

Here for an object Y, its support∥Y∥ is the the image of the unique map Y→1.
