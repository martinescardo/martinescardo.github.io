One model of intuionistic propositional logic is the open sets of a topological space X. There false is ∅, true is X, "or" is union, and "not" is interior of the complement. For example, in the Euclidean line, ¬(-∞,0)=(0,∞) and so ¬(-∞,0) ∪ ¬(-∞,0) = ℝ\{0} ≠ ℝ = true.
