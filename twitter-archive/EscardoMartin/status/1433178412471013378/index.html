* A space is totally separated iff the clopens separate the points.

We'll remain classical-topological until otherwise stated.

Martin Escardo

@EscardoMartin

Definition. A type X is called *compact* iff there is a function A:(X→2)→2 such that A(p)=1 ⇔ p(x)=1 for all x:X.

This notion of compactness will only resemble the classical notion of compactness when X is a totally separated type.

(We should say 2-compact in general.)

Theorem.
* Totally separated types are sets (in the HoTT/UF sense).
* There is a universal way to transform any type into a totally separated type.
* A type is compact iff its totally separated reflection is compact.

Martin Escardo

@EscardoMartin

Some closure properties.

* A finite product of compact types is compact.
* A product of pointed compact types indexed by a subsingleton is compact.
* A disjoint union of compact types with a compact index type is compact.
* Images of compact types are compact.

* If X is totally separated, Y is disconnected, and (X→Y) is compact, then X is discrete.
