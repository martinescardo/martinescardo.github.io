There is nothing mysterious about "canonical isomorphism". Sometimes it is more convenient to work with A and sometimes with B, in the same mathematical development, where we have established that A and B are isomorphic in a particular way. There may be other isomorphisms.

But we are only interested in this particular one. We could call it "the isomorphism that we have established and that we are going to use from now on to switch between A and B". This is too long, and "canonical isomorphism" is a shorthand for that.
