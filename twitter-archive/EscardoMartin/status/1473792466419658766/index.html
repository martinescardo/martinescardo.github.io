@EscardoMartin

If countable choice is available, in which case the Dedekind and Cauchy reals agree, the Dedekind propositions are precisely the semi-decidable propositions, where the semi-decidable propositions are those of the form (∃ n : ℕ , A n) with A n decidable for every n : ℕ.
