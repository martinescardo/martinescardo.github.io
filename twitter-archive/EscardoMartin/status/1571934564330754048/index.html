Martin Escardo

@EscardoMartin

Andre Joyal discovered, in the 1970's, two nice symmetries, both of which are classically invisible.

(i) One of them is a dual to the notion of compactness.

(ii) The other is a dual to the Hausdorff property.

(ii) Joyal also defined a locale to be discrete if its diagonal is open.
Now in classical mathematics, as soon as the diagonal is open, it is also closed, and hence the locale is also Hausdorff. This matches our experience of classical topology: discrete spaces are Hausdorff.
