Let ℕ_∞ be the one-point compactification of the discrete space ℕ of natural numbers. Then a sequence x_i in a space X has a limit ℓ if and only if the (trivially continuous) map x:ℕ → X extends to a continuous map x̂:ℕ_∞ → X with x̂(∞)=ℓ.
