HoTT/UF is radically different from ZFC and relatives:
(i) The primitive mathematical objects are different (types rather than sets)
(ii) The way logic works is different (even for *classical* HoTT/UF with excluded middle and choice).
(iii) The way equality works is different.

Martin Escardo

@EscardoMartin

Types are classified according to their homotopy levels:
(i) -2: contractible.
(ii) -1: propositions. (Mathematical statements.)
(iii) 0: sets. (E.g. that of natural numbers.)
(iv) 1: 1-groupoids (E.g. that of 1-categories.)
(v) 2: 2-groupoids (E.g. that of bicategories).
