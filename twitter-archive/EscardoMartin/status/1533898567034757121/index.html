Let 2 be the two-point discrete space {0,1}, and let X be a totally separated space in your chosen category.

This means that for any two distinct points x and y of X, there is a clopen set containg x but not y.
(https://encyclopediaofmath.org/wiki/Totally_separated_space)

Let 2^X be the exponential (internal hom) in your chosen cartesian closed category of topological spaces.

Then 2^X is the set of clopen sets of X with a suitable topology, which is uniquely determined by the cartesian closedness requirement.
