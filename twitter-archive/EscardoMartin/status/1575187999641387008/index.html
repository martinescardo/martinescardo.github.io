In classical mathematics, we can define a Dedekind real to be a set L of rational numbers such that
(i) L has at least one rational number as a member,
(ii) if q is in L and p<q then p is in L, and
(iii) if q is in L then there is p>q in L.

In constructive mathematics, a Dedekind real is defined to be a pair (L,U) of sets of rational numbers such that L satisfies (i-iii) and U satisfies the dual axioms (i'-iii'), and
(iv) if p is in L and q is in U, then p<q, and
(v) if p<q, then either p is in L or q is in U.

Martin Escardo

@EscardoMartin

In the topos of sheaves over a topological space X,
(A) two-sided reals = sheaf of continuous real valued functions on X.
(B) lower reals = sheaf of lower semicontinuous real valued functions on X.
(C) upper reals = sheaf of upper semicontinuous real valued functions on X.
