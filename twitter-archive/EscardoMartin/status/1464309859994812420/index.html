People have been discussing the Cantor-Schröder-Bernstein Theorem for discrete sets (sets with decidable equality) in constructive mathematics.
If there is a function that maps any two given injections X → Y and Y → X of discrete sets to a bijection X → Y, then LEM follows.

What is known, at least implicitly, is that CSB for discrete sets, with existence rather than a function, is not provable in constructive mathematics.

This a direct consequence of Lemma D.4.1.2 of Johnstone's Sketches of an Elephant: A Topos Theory Compendium.
